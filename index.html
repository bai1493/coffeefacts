Here are the four basic steps to enjoying coffee flavour no matter what preparation method you use.

  • The first step is to smell a coffee before tasting it, as smell is corelated to how we taste. This is the first step because your nose can distinguish between thousands of smells, while your mouth can only detect four tastes - salty, sweet, bitter and sour. Cup your hand over the top of the cup, bring it up to your nose and inhale.
  • Second, you want to make sure you slurp it. This will spray the coffee across your whole mouth and ensures the subtle flavours are spread across your entire palate. It also lets the flavours and aromas reach all of your tasting zones.
  • Next, you'll want to locate where you feel the flavours on your tongue. What are you experiencing?
  • Last, think of some words you would consider using to describe the tastes and feelings you are experiencing. Does the coffee flavours and aromas remind you of similar food profiles?

These are the four main steps to enjoying the differences between coffee flavours, no matter the method used to prepare it!
